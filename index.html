Common New Hire Challenges & Solutions
  • Challenge: Information Overload → Solution: Focus on one thing at a time. Master case management first, then add product knowledge incrementally. Don't try to learn everything Week 1
  • Challenge: Imposter Syndrome → Solution: Everyone feels this way starting out. Ask questions freely - team expects this. Your fresh perspective is valuable
  • Challenge: Fear of Customer Calls → Solution: Start with email-only cases. Listen to senior engineers' calls. Practice with mentor in role-play before taking real calls
  • Challenge: Not Knowing When to Escalate → Solution: Ask mentor "Would you escalate this?" Better to escalate early than waste hours stuck. Document what you tried first
  • Challenge: Remembering All the Tools/Processes → Solution: Create your own cheat sheet/quick reference. Bookmark important pages. Repetition builds muscle memory

2. Complex System Troubleshooting
Diagnose and resolve intricate hardware, software, and integration issues in process control systems under time pressure.
Methodology:
  • Gather complete information: config, errors, recent changes, timeline
  • Use divide-and-conquer: isolate network, controller, I/O, field devices
  • Check basics first: power, connectivity, cables, grounding
  • Use diagnostic tools: DeltaV Diagnostics, AMS, network analyzers
  • Review logs and alarms for patterns
  • Replicate in test environment when possible
  • Know when to escalate to specialists

4. Is regulatory compliance violated? → YES = P1

IS THIS P2 (HIGH)?

2. Is workaround unsustainable for >24 hours? → YES = P2
3. Will this become P1 within 24 hours if not fixed? → YES = P2
4. Are multiple units/processes affected? → YES = P2
If ALL answers are NO → P3 or P4

Root Cause Analysis Flow
FINDING ROOT CAUSE - 5 WHYS METHOD
Example: Controller communication lost
Problem: Controller shows offline
Why 1: Why is controller offline? → Network connection lost
Why 2: Why was network lost? → Network cable unplugged
Why 3: Why was cable unplugged? → Maintenance work in area
Why 4: Why during maintenance? → No notification to operations
Why 5: Why no notification? → No formal communication process
ROOT CAUSE: Lack of maintenance communication procedure
FIX: Implement maintenance notification process (prevents recurrence)

START: Controller shows red X or offline

Field Device Troubleshooting Flow
STEP-BY-STEP: DEVICE BAD QUALITY/OFFLINE
START: Field device shows bad quality or not responding
↓
Step 1: Check power - Is 24VDC present at device terminals?
→ NO: Fix power supply | YES: Go to Step 2
↓
Step 2: Measure loop resistance (HART: should be <1000Ω)
→ Out of range: Check wiring, termination resistor | OK: Go to Step 3
↓
Step 3: Open junction box - Look for water, corrosion, loose wires
→ Found issues: Clean/dry/tighten | Clean: Go to Step 4
↓
Step 4: Use HART Communicator or AMS - Can you communicate directly?
→ YES: Issue is upstream (I/O card, network) | NO: Go to Step 5
↓
Step 5: Check device address (HART polling address)
→ Wrong address: Reconfigure | Correct: Device likely failed
↓
Step 6: Swap with known good device
→ Works: Original device defective, arrange RMA | Fails: Check I/O card

Customer Industry Deep Dives
Understanding your customer's processes helps you provide better, faster support.
Oil & Gas Industry
REFINERY PROCESS UNITS
Crude Distillation Unit (CDU): Separates crude oil into fractions by boiling point. Critical controls: Temperature, pressure, level in column. Why it matters: CDU downtime stops entire refinery.

Fluid Catalytic Cracking (FCC): Converts heavy oil to gasoline/diesel. High temperature (500-700°C), complex reactions. Critical: Reactor temperature control, catalyst circulation. Safety risk if runaway reaction.

Hydrocracker: Uses hydrogen to break molecules. High pressure (up to 2000 psi), explosive hydrogen present. Safety critical: Pressure relief systems, hydrogen leak detection.

Alkylation Unit: Uses sulfuric acid (highly corrosive). Critical controls: Acid concentration, temperature. Safety: Leak detection, emergency shutdown systems mandatory.
OIL & GAS SAFETY CONCERNS
Explosive atmospheres: Hydrocarbons + air = explosion risk. All equipment must be intrinsically safe/explosion proof.
H2S (Hydrogen Sulfide): Deadly gas, even low concentrations. Always carry H2S monitor, never work alone.
High pressure: Sudden release can be fatal. Never approach leaking equipment.
SIS (Safety Instrumented Systems): Never bypass safety interlocks. Understand SIL ratings (SIL 1/2/3).
Philippine context: Petron Bataan refinery, Shell Tabangao - major facilities requiring highest safety standards.
Power Generation Industry
POWER PLANT SYSTEMS
Boiler Controls: Combustion control (fuel + air ratio), steam temperature/pressure. Critical: Flame monitoring, drum level control. Why: Boiler explosions are catastrophic.

Turbine Controls: Speed/load control, vibration monitoring, bearing temperature. Critical: Overspeed protection (turbines can disintegrate), emergency trip systems.

Generator Protection: Excitation control, synchronization, grid connection. Critical: Reverse power, loss of excitation, differential protection relays.

Emissions Monitoring: NOx, SOx, particulates - regulatory compliance. Philippine DOE: Strict emission limits, continuous monitoring required, penalties for violations.
POWER INDUSTRY CONCERNS
Grid stability: Philippines has island grids (Luzon, Visayas, Mindanao). Frequency control critical (60 Hz ±0.2 Hz).
Forced outages: Every minute offline = revenue loss + penalties. P1 issues 24/7.
Cycling duty: Plants start/stop frequently. Equipment wear, controls must handle transients.
Key customers: Meralco PowerGen (coal, gas), First Gen (gas, geothermal), Aboitiz (coal, hydro, renewables).
Manufacturing Industry (Food, Pharma, Chemical)
BATCH VS CONTINUOUS PROCESSES
Batch Processing (Food, Pharma): Recipe-driven, specific sequence of operations. Example: Mix ingredients → Heat → Cool → Package. Each batch is tracked (lot number). DeltaV Batch: Recipe management, equipment arbitration, batch history critical.

Continuous Processing (Chemicals, Oil): Steady-state operation, 24/7 production. Example: Continuous polymerization, chemical synthesis. Focus: Maintaining setpoints, quality consistency, equipment reliability.
FOOD & PHARMA REGULATIONS
FDA 21 CFR Part 11 (Philippines FDA follows US FDA): Electronic records/signatures must be secure, traceable, auditable. DeltaV Impact: Electronic signature required for recipe changes, audit trail immutable, user access controls strict.

HACCP (Hazard Analysis Critical Control Points): Food safety system. Critical Control Points: Cooking temperature, cooling time, metal detection. Control system role: Monitor/alarm on critical parameters.

GMP (Good Manufacturing Practice): Quality assurance. Requirements: Equipment qualification (IQ/OQ/PQ), change control, deviation management. Support impact: Any system change requires extensive documentation/approval.

Key PH customers: Nestle (coffee, dairy), San Miguel (beverage, food), Universal Robina (snacks), Unilab (pharma).
Semiconductor & Electronics
CLEANROOM ENVIRONMENT CONTROLS
Cleanroom Classification: Class 1 to Class 100,000 (particles per cubic foot). Semiconductor = Class 1-10 (ultra-clean). Controls: Air filtration (HEPA/ULPA), temperature (±0.5°C), humidity (±2% RH), pressure differential (positive pressure to prevent contamination ingress).

Process Equipment Integration: Fab tools (lithography, etch, deposition) controlled by DeltaV or interfaced via MES/SECS-GEM. Challenge: Millisecond precision, recipe management, equipment tracking.

Yield Impact: Any contamination = scrapped wafers. Multi-million dollar impact. Control system reliability: Absolutely critical, redundancy required.

PH Semiconductor: Texas Instruments (Clark), Analog Devices (Cavite), TDK (Laguna). High-tech, demanding customers, expect world-class support.
Mining Industry
MINING PROCESS OVERVIEW
Crushing & Grinding: Ore broken down to fine particles. Controls: Motor load, crusher gap, conveyor speed. Heavy duty equipment, vibration monitoring critical.

Flotation: Separates valuable minerals from waste rock using chemicals and air bubbles. Controls: pH, reagent dosing, air flow, froth level. Complex chemistry, environmental concerns.

Tailings Management: Waste material disposal. Environmental critical: Prevents dam failures, water contamination. Level monitoring, flow control, emergency systems.

Remote Sites: Many PH mines in remote locations (Benguet, Surigao, Palawan). Satellite/radio communication, harsh environment, limited site access. Remote support essential.

Key customers: Philex Mining (Benguet), Nickel Asia (various sites). Environmental regulations strict post-Marcopper incident.
Water & Wastewater Treatment
WATER TREATMENT PROCESSES
Drinking Water: Coagulation → Flocculation → Sedimentation → Filtration → Disinfection (chlorine). Critical controls: Chlorine dosing (too little = bacteria, too much = toxic), pH, turbidity. Regulatory: DOH drinking water standards, continuous monitoring.

Wastewater Treatment: Screening → Primary settling → Biological treatment (activated sludge) → Secondary settling → Disinfection. Controls: Dissolved oxygen (aeration), MLSS (mixed liquor suspended solids), sludge wasting rate. Challenge: Variable influent, biological process sensitive.

SCADA Systems: Multiple remote sites (pump stations, reservoirs). Communications: Radio, cellular, sometimes unreliable in PH. Alarm management, data historization for compliance reporting.

Key customers: Manila Water (east zone), Maynilad (west zone). Service millions, 24/7 operations, regulatory compliance critical (DENR, LGU).
